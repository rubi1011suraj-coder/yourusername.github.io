🌌 Black Holes 🌌

What is a Black Hole?

A black hole is a region in space where gravity is so strong that nothing can escape from it, not even light.

How is a Black Hole Formed?

Black holes are usually formed when a very massive star runs out of fuel and collapses under its own gravity.

Interesting Facts

  • Nothing can escape after crossing the event horizon.
  • Black holes can bend space and time.
  • Scientists have photographed a black hole.
  • Supermassive black holes exist at the centers of many galaxies.

Conclusion

Black holes are among the most mysterious objects in the universe. Scientists continue to study them to better understand how our universe works.
